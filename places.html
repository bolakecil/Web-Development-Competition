POPULAR RESORTS IN NTT




Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Respect for Elders: In Indonesian culture, including NTT, respect for elders is highly valued. It's customary to greet and show deference to older individuals.
  2. Modesty in Dress: Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or rural areas. Wearing conservative clothing demonstrates respect for local customs.
  3. Greetings and Politeness: Learn some basic Indonesian greetings and polite phrases. Saying "Selamat Pagi" (Good morning), "Selamat Siang" (Good afternoon), or "Selamat Malam" (Good evening) is appreciated.
  4. Shoes off Indoors: It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone's home, and sometimes even in certain businesses or establishments. Pay attention to cues from locals.
  5. Gift Giving: If invited to someone's home, it's a nice gesture to bring a small gift, such as snacks or a token from your home country. Present and receive gifts with both hands.
  1. Vaccinations: Ensure your routine vaccinations are up-to-date. Depending on your travel plans, consider vaccinations for diseases such as Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and Japanese Encephalitis.
  2. Malaria Prevention: NTT is in a malaria-prone region. Consult with a healthcare professional about antimalarial medication and take measures to prevent mosquito bites, such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves.
  3. Food and Water Safety: Consume only properly cooked and hot foods. Avoid street food that may pose sanitation risks. Drink bottled or treated water, and avoid ice in your drinks.
  4. Sun Protection: The tropical climate can be intense. Use sunscreen, wear a hat, and stay hydrated to protect yourself from the sun.
  5. Medical Facilities: Be aware of the available medical facilities in the area you are visiting. In remote areas, medical facilities may be limited, so carry a basic first aid kit.
  6. Travel Insurance: Purchase comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, evacuation, and unexpected events. Check the coverage details and contact information in case of emergencies.
  7. Transportation Safety: Exercise caution when using local transportation. Choose reputable operators and follow safety guidelines. Roads in some areas may be challenging, so plan your journeys accordingly.
  8. Weather Awareness: Stay informed about local weather conditions, especially if you plan outdoor activities. NTT can experience tropical storms and heavy rainfall during certain seasons.
  1. Public Buses: Public buses are a common mode of transportation for both short and long distances. They connect major towns and cities within the region.
  2. Shared Minivans (Bemos): Bemos are small minivans that operate on fixed routes. They are a popular and affordable means of transport, especially in rural areas.
  3. Taxis: Taxis are available in urban areas and can be hailed on the street or booked through mobile apps. Confirm the fare before starting your journey.
  4. Ojek: Ojeks are motorcycle taxis that provide a convenient way to navigate through traffic. Negotiate the fare before getting on an ojek.
  5. Rental Cars: Rental cars are available in major cities, providing flexibility for exploring the region independently. Ensure you have a valid driver's license and consider road conditions.
  6. Walking: In towns and cities, walking is a common and enjoyable way to explore local attractions. Be mindful of local traffic and pedestrian safety.
