Rumpu Rampe

The name itself translates to "many" or "diverse," reflecting the essence of this dish. It features a vibrant combinations of seasonal vegetables stir-fried together. Ingredients include: leafy greens such as water spinach, papaya leaves; and starchy options like daun singkong (cassava leaves) and sometimes even jagung (corn).

Kolo

A unique culinary experience from the Manggarai region of East Nusa Tenggara (NTT) province in Indonesia. It's essentially baked rice, cooked inside a bamboo tube over an open fire. The resulting dish is known for its smoky flavor and fluffy texture.

Sei Sapi

Delicious smoked beef dish originating from NTT. The beef is seasoned with various spices and herbs, then slow-smoked over hot coals for several hours. This process imbues the meat with a rich, smoky flavor and a tender texture.

Ikan Kuah Belimbing

Translates to "fish in starfruit soup." A common ingredient in Indonesian cuisine. This dish features fish cooked in a flavorful broth flavored with starfruit, vegetables, and spices. The starfruit adds a touch of tartness and a unique depth of flavor to the soup.

Kue Alu Ngene

Sweet treat from NTT. It's a type of fried fritter made with grated cassava (alu) and ndene, a local term for palm sugar. The combination of cassava and palm sugar creates a crispy exterior and a sweet, chewy interior.
